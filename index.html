About

Langton's Anthill is a 2-dimensional cellular automaton based on Langton's Ant.

But instead of the original two states (on/off, alive/dead), cell on the anthill have 255 states. Accordingly, the ants follow a list of 255 left/right rules. Every ant has a randomly generated, more or less unique set of rules it follows while hopping from cell to cell.

In the simulation the state of each cell is represented by a shade of grey where white represents the state 0 and black the state 255. If a cell has state 255 and gets increased once more it flips to state 0.

What can I do?

By clicking on the screen you can drop ants on the anthill. Adjust the drop area and the amount of ants per drop with the sliders above.

Then just lean back and enjoy the tiny little ants crawling over your screen. They build structures that look organic but not random. They build highways between structures and follow them. They meet and seperate. Sometimes they travel together. I even saw them dancing.
